Business Analysis Process

A structured, end-to-end BA approach for the Retail Operations Backoffice case study

Project Timeline & Phases

01

Discovery & Analysis

Focus: goals, stakeholders, constraints, current-state pain points

  • Define business outcomes (variance reduction, faster reporting)
  • Identify actors and decision rights (approvals / overrides)
  • Document core entities and audit events
02

Requirements Definition

Focus: functional flows + NFRs + business rules

  • Write FRs per module and end-to-end scenarios
  • Capture NFRs (auditability, performance, security)
  • Define decision tables (promotions, stock rules)
03

Modeling & Validation Prep

Focus: make flows consistent and testable

  • Align UC → sequence → activity per module
  • Confirm edge cases (returns, negative stock, cancellations)
  • Prepare traceability (requirements → tests)
04

UAT & Sign-off

Focus: acceptance-ready evidence

  • UAT test cases in a recruiter-friendly XLSX template
  • RTM coverage checks
  • UAT summary and sign-off readiness

Stakeholder Analysis

Business Owner

Interest: High | Power: High

Key Concerns: shrinkage control, profitability, reporting

Branch Manager

Interest: High | Power: Medium

Key Concerns: approvals, overrides, daily operations

Cashier / Inventory Staff

Interest: High | Power: Low

Key Concerns: speed, usability, clear exception handling

IT / Delivery Team

Interest: Medium | Power: High

Key Concerns: clear rules, feasibility, testability

This project naturally fits a role-based access control model (RBAC) with approvals and audit events.

Requirements Elicitation Techniques

Stakeholder Interviews

Understand roles, approvals, and operational constraints across branches.

Workshops

Align on scope, prioritize modules, and resolve promo/stock rule conflicts.

Observation

Follow day-to-day flows: receiving, transfers, cash closure, stocktake.

Document & Diagram Review

Consolidate existing UC/sequence/activity diagrams into a consistent model.

Process Modeling & Analysis

AS-IS (Common Reality)

Typical pain points:

  • Spreadsheet-driven tracking with inconsistent updates
  • Manual approvals/overrides without clear audit evidence
  • Promo conflicts and unclear exception handling
  • Delayed reporting and reconciliation

TO-BE (Defined Target Process)

Process model: BPMN L0 (PNG)

Improvements:

  • Standardized workflows per module
  • Decision points and exceptions made explicit
  • RBAC boundaries and approvals
  • Requirements-to-UAT traceability
