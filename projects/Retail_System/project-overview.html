Retail Operations Backoffice

Understanding the business problem and a practical backoffice solution scope

My Role & What I Did

Role: Business Analyst (case study). I shaped an MVP backoffice scope around controls and auditability, then produced artifacts a dev + QA team could use for implementation and validation.

  • Clarified business rules and approval points (stock, promo, pricing, cashbook)
  • Modeled flows and actors (BPMN + use cases + sequence/activity diagrams)
  • Created traceability and test evidence (RTM + UAT pack)
  • Added SQL samples to demonstrate data logic and reporting queries
Evidence you can verify: Deliverables include RTM-to-UAT mapping, decision tables, and a SQL showcase pack (see Deliverables).

The Business Problem

High Impact

Many small and mid-sized retail stores still run daily operations with spreadsheets and informal procedures. The result is inconsistent data, weak controls, and slow decision-making.

Stock Variance

Frequent mismatches between system stock and physical count

Adjustments lack consistent reason codes and approvals

Weak Audit Trail

Hard to trace who changed price/stock/promo and why

Limited accountability across branches

Promo / Pricing Inconsistency

Conflicting promotions and manual overrides

Inconsistent application across branches

Slow Reporting

Late visibility into sales, inventory, and cashbook status

Decisions made with incomplete information

The Proposed Solution

Defined Scope

Process map: BPMN L0 (PNG)

Standardized Backoffice Workflows

  • Clear end-to-end flows across core modules
  • Defined actors/permissions and decision points
  • Audit-friendly actions (reason codes, approvals)
  • Consistent documentation for onboarding

Controls That Feel “Real”

  • RBAC boundaries for cashier / inventory / manager / admin
  • Traceability from requirements → UAT
  • Business rules captured in decision tables

Project Scope & Boundaries

IN SCOPE (MVP Modules)

  • Sales (checkout, invoices, payments)
  • Inventory (stocktake, adjustments, reconciliation)
  • Purchasing (receiving, supplier invoices, returns)
  • Transfers (inter-branch transfers + receiving)
  • Promotions (setup, validation, application logic)
  • Cashbook (cash-in/out, end-of-day closure)
  • Reporting (summary dashboards)

OUT OF SCOPE

  • POS hardware integration
  • E-commerce / omnichannel fulfillment
  • Advanced forecasting and ML analytics
  • Third-party accounting system integrations
  • Multi-country tax localization
