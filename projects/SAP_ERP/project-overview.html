SAP ERP (Mock) — BA Case Study

Simulated BA engagement: Order-to-Cash (O2C) with approvals, pricing, and auditability (example reference: SAP S/4HANA)

Key Artifacts

BPMN L0 Stakeholder Matrix SQL Samples BA Checklist Rules / Decision Tables RTM UAT Pack

Objective

Build a recruiter-friendly BA case study for a mock SAP ERP Order-to-Cash (O2C) scope and produce a complete, interview-ready deliverables pack (requirements, rules, RTM, and UAT). To make the mock concrete, terminology aligns to an example reference platform: SAP S/4HANA (SD + FI-AR + FSCM Credit Management). This is still ERP-mock and vendor-proprietary content is not used.

The Business Problem

High Impact

In an Order-to-Cash process, the biggest operational risks are usually not “happy-path automation”, but controls (credit, pricing approvals) and auditability (who changed what, when, and why). ERP implementations often fail when rules are undocumented, approvals are unclear, and testing coverage is weak.

Pricing & Discount Risk

Uncontrolled overrides create margin leakage

Discounts and manual price changes need rules + approvals

Credit & Compliance

Credit limit breaches increase bad debt exposure

Credit checks, blocks, and release workflows must be explicit

Exception Handling

Cancellations/returns are where processes break

Need consistent rules for delivery, billing, and reversal paths

Auditability & Traceability

Hard to prove decisions and approvals

Requirements must map cleanly to tests and sign-off evidence

The Proposed Solution

Defined Scope

Process map: BPMN L0 (PNG)

Order-to-Cash (O2C) Scenario

  • Sales order → delivery → billing → payment
  • Credit checks, blocks, and approval releases
  • Pricing overrides and discount governance
  • Exception paths: cancellations, returns, reversals

Governance & Traceability

  • RBAC + approvals with decision tables
  • RTM linking requirements ↔ tests
  • UAT evidence: test cases + summary + sign-off

Project Scope & Boundaries

IN SCOPE

  • O2C steps: sales order, delivery, billing, payment posting
  • Credit management controls (checks, blocks, releases)
  • Pricing/discount governance (rules + approvals)
  • Audit events and traceability expectations
  • UAT-ready deliverables pack (XLSX + sign-off summary)

OUT OF SCOPE

  • Real SAP configuration / screenshots / proprietary artifacts
  • Full finance closing, tax localization, and treasury scope
  • Real integration interfaces (EDI/IDoc) implementation details
  • Performance testing at enterprise scale
